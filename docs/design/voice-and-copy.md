# TravelPins Voice and Copy

## Voice

Short, warm, observant, and conversational. TravelPins should sound like a friend
who knows the map and makes choosing easier, not a concierge, critic, or algorithm.

## Rules

- Write one thought per line.
- Prefer familiar words and contractions.
- Invite rather than command.
- Describe a human reason to return, not a feature.
- Keep interface labels self-contained.
- Remove copy that repeats the heading or button.
- Use sentence case. Avoid exclamation marks and marketing claims.
- Keep the website UI in English.

## Product Vocabulary

Prefer:

- place
- next stop
- saved
- visited
- return
- from your map
- another one

Avoid:

- destination intelligence
- recommendation engine
- authenticated or approved
- endless shortlist
- hidden gem
- must-visit

## Picker Flow

| Stage | Personal map | Nearby discovery |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Entry | `Where to next?` | `Where to next?` |
| Source | `From my map` | `Somewhere new` |
| Setup | `What sounds good?` | `A place not on your map yet.` |
| Commit | `Choose for me` | `Find a place` |
| Result | `Try this one` | `Try somewhere new` |
| Next action | `Show on map` | `Open directions` |
| Repeat | `Another one` | `Try another place` |

The entry names the user's question. The preference screen asks for mood. The
commit explicitly delegates the decision. The result offers rather than declares.
`Somewhere new` means not yet present in this TravelPins map; never claim to know
every place the person has visited in real life.

## Place Memories

Lead with the reason to return. Use concrete, human details. Keep list memories to
one calm line; preserve the full note in the place detail.

Good: `A comfortable CBD pause with a solid cup and plenty of room.`

Avoid: `Highly recommended cafe with excellent amenities and premium coffee.`
